Use these as the new official Academy question flows.
They are replacing the previous longer set.

The goal of this new version is to:

reduce completion fatigue

improve answer quality

reduce duplication across roles

make outputs easier to turn into story blueprints

keep strong emotional and football-specific depth without inviting overly sensitive disclosures

Important build rules:

Keep the wording of the questions very close to what is written below.

Use adaptive logic where noted.

Keep free-text boxes generous but not intimidating.

Use multiselect and select fields where marked.

Keep the UI clean, premium, and easy to complete.

Show progress clearly.

Save answers as structured data so they can later feed a story matrix and author workflow.

Player journey and coach input are core.

Staff roles are package-dependent.

Parent, friend, and external contributors are optional invite-only layers.

NEW OFFICIAL QUESTION SET
1) PLAYER JOURNEY — Older Players

Target: 24 core questions max, plus one position branch and a small number of adaptive follow-ups.

Stage 1 — Your World

Pick the words that describe you best as a person.
Response type: multiselect

Tell me about one football moment you always go back to when you remember why you love the game.
Response type: long text

Describe the way you play in your own words — not your position, your game.
Response type: long text

Who do you usually tell first when something good happens in football, and what do they normally say?
Response type: short text

Is there anything at home that means something to you about football — a shirt, boots, notebook, photo, anything like that? Why does it matter?
Response type: long text

Adaptive only if second position is selected:

When you switch positions, what changes most in the way you think or play?
Response type: long text

Stage 2 — Your Football Mind

Show one position-specific scenario only, based on the player's main position.

GK:
A striker is through on goal and your defender is recovering. What are you reading, and what is your decision?

CB:
The striker drops deep to receive. What tells you whether to step in or hold your line?

FB (LB/RB):
Your winger has the ball ahead of you in attack. What tells you whether to overlap, underlap, or hold your position?

CDM:
The opposition break through the middle and you are the last midfielder back. What are you looking at first, and how do you deal with it?

CM:
Your holding midfielder is under pressure in build-up. Where do you move, and what are you trying to give the team?

CAM:
You are about to receive between the lines. What are you checking before the ball arrives, and what are you hoping to create?

Winger:
You are 1v1 out wide. What tells you whether to go outside, come inside, or wait for support?

ST/CF:
Your team wins the ball and breaks quickly. What is your first movement, and what are you trying to open up?

Position scenario response type: long text

Then ask all players:

Your team is 1–0 up with ten minutes left. What changes in the way you play?
Response type: long text

Before your first touch arrives under pressure, what are you already checking or knowing?
Response type: long text

If a teammate keeps getting a positional detail wrong in a game, how do you handle it?
Response type: select plus optional short follow-up text: “What would you actually say?”

When you are not at your best personally, how do you still help the team?
Response type: long text

Stage 3 — The Work Nobody Sees

Tell me about a time you were disappointed in football — maybe not being picked, not starting, or feeling overlooked. What happened?
Response type: long text

What do you do for your game that most people do not see?
Response type: long text

When you feel overlooked or undervalued, what do you usually do next?
Response type: select plus short follow-up

What is one quality in your game that people often miss?
Response type: short text

Who at this club really understands you — not just your football, but you?
Response type: short text

Stage 4 — Tough Periods

Tell me about a difficult period in football that really tested you. What made it hard?
Response type: long text

During that period, what were you thinking or feeling most often?
Response type: long text

What does pressure usually look like in you during games — in your body, your thoughts, or your behaviour?
Response type: long text

What helped you keep going during that period?
Response type: multiselect

Was there anyone who helped you through it, even in a small way? What did they do?
Response type: long text

Stage 5 — The Turn

Was there a moment, conversation, or decision that started to change things for you? Tell me about it.
Response type: long text

What actually shifted that helped you move forward?
Response type: multiselect

Describe the first session or match where you felt more like yourself again. What was different?
Response type: long text

Looking back now, what do you understand that you could not see at the time?
Response type: long text

Stage 6 — Who You Are

What are your strongest qualities as a player and as a person?
Response type: multiselect

What is something true about you that people do not always see straight away?
Response type: long text

What would your closest teammates say about you away from the pitch?
Response type: long text

What keeps you going in football when things are hard?
Response type: long text

What do you know about yourself now that you would not want to lose?
Response type: long text

Stage 7 — The Chapter Ahead

What do you want to achieve this season, on and off the pitch?
Response type: multiselect plus optional text

Where do you dream of playing one day?
Response type: multiselect

What kind of player and person do you want to become over the next few years?
Response type: long text

What do you hope people say about you that has nothing to do with stats or trophies?
Response type: long text

If this chapter of your football life had a title, what would it be?
Response type: short text

Data and product rules

Save every answer with metadata: question ID, stage, role, age group, timestamp, and player ID.

Structure the data so it can later feed a story blueprint generator and author matrix.

Support draft save and return later.

Show progress by stage, not just by total percentage.

Allow admin users to toggle which optional stakeholder forms are enabled.

Use gentle helper text before sensitive-adjacent sections:
“Please keep answers focused on football, learning, character, and general observations. Do not include medical details, safeguarding disclosures, or confidential family information.”

Keep the UI premium, simple, and emotionally warm.

Avoid making the form feel clinical or like an assessment test.
