create a forced-choice player mindset profiler instead of simple self-rating sliders.

The user should see pairs of statements and must choose which one is more like them.

Then the system converts those choices into trait scores behind the scenes.

A very good format is:

Option A ← slider / toggle → Option B

Example:
I like taking charge ——— I like making sure everyone feels included

They must lean one way or the other.

You can make it a 5-point forced slider:

Strongly A

Slightly A

In between

Slightly B

Strongly B

That is much better than “rate yourself from 1 to 5.”

Why this works better

Because both options sound positive, the child cannot just keep choosing the “best” answer every time.

You are forcing trade-offs:

confidence vs caution

discipline vs creativity

team-first vs individual expression

persistence vs adaptability

leadership vs support role

That reveals how they think.

Best trait dimensions for this type of system

For your platform, I would measure these 6:

Drive

Discipline

Resilience

Team Orientation

Leadership Style

Learning Style

Not as labels shown to the user at first, but as internal scoring categories.

The best format

Each question should secretly map to one of two traits.

For example:

A-choice adds points to Drive

B-choice adds points to Team Orientation

After 12–18 questions, you calculate the profile.

Example forced-choice questions you can plug in

Here are strong ones.

1. Ambition vs enjoyment

Which feels more like you?

I want to become one of the best players I can be.

I want football to stay fun and enjoyable no matter what.

Reveals:

competitive drive vs enjoyment orientation

2. Leadership vs support

I like being the one who leads and speaks up.

I like helping the team quietly through my actions.

Reveals:

visible leadership vs quiet leadership

3. Persistence vs reset

When I have a bad session, I want to fix it immediately.

When I have a bad session, I do better after stepping back and resetting.

Reveals:

intense persistence vs reflective recovery

4. Discipline vs freedom

I like having a clear plan to follow.

I like having freedom to express myself in my own way.

Reveals:

structure preference vs freedom preference

5. Recognition vs contribution

I enjoy it when people notice what I did well.

I am happy when I know I helped, even if no one notices.

Reveals:

external recognition vs internal/team contribution

6. Challenge vs security

I enjoy being pushed into difficult situations.

I do better when I feel settled and confident first.

Reveals:

challenge-seeking vs stability-seeking

7. Bounce-back style

After mistakes, I push myself harder.

After mistakes, I need to calm myself before I can improve.

Reveals:

aggressive recovery vs emotional regulation style

8. Team vs self-expression

I would rather do what helps the team most.

I would rather play in a way that shows what I can do.

Reveals:

team-first vs self-expression

9. Learning style

I learn best when someone explains exactly what to do.

I learn best by trying things and figuring them out.

Reveals:

coached learning vs experiential learning

10. Pressure response

Pressure makes me want the ball more.

Pressure makes me focus on doing the simple things well.

Reveals:

spotlight confidence vs composed pragmatism

11. Motivation source

I am motivated most by big goals.

I am motivated most by daily improvement.

Reveals:

outcome focus vs process focus

12. Setback meaning

A setback makes me want to prove people wrong.

A setback makes me want to understand what I need to improve.

Reveals:

competitive response vs reflective growth response

A better way to display it

Do not show the result as:

“You are lazy”

“You lack confidence”

Instead show balanced spectrum sliders such as:

Big-picture ambition ←→ Love of the game

Vocal leadership ←→ Quiet example

Structured discipline ←→ Creative freedom

Immediate response ←→ Reflective reset

Recognition seeking ←→ Quiet contribution

Coach-led learning ←→ Self-discovery learning

That is much more intelligent and more respectful.

Best hidden scoring model

Behind the scenes, each answer gives points to a category.

For example:

Question:
“I like having a clear plan to follow” vs “I like having freedom to express myself in my own way”

Scoring:

Strongly A = +2 Discipline

Slightly A = +1 Discipline

Middle = 0

Slightly B = +1 Creativity

Strongly B = +2 Creativity

Do this across all questions and aggregate the results.
