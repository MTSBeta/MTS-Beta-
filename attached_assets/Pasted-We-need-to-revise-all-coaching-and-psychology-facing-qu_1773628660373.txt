We need to revise all coaching and psychology-facing questions across the platform using a clearer design standard.

What changed from the old questions to the new questions

The old questions were thoughtful, but they leaned too far into:

abstract interpretation

mind-reading

emotionally loaded wording

poetic phrasing

broad psychological conclusions

The new questions should be better because they are:

1. More observable

Old questions often asked the respondent to explain the player’s inner world directly.

New questions should ask for:

behaviour

body language

communication

decision-making

repeated patterns

specific situations

We want staff to answer from what they have actually seen, not what they are guessing.

2. More evidence-based

Old questions invited broad interpretations like:

“what does that tell you about who they are?”

“what shaped them?”

“what does their inner dialogue look like?”

New questions should instead ask:

what happened?

when does this usually happen?

what pattern have you noticed?

what example best shows this?

That gives us stronger, more usable data.

3. Less clinical or therapist-like

Some of the old wording sounded like the respondent was being asked to diagnose or psychoanalyse the child.

The new version should sound more like:

developmental observation

performance psychology

coaching insight

reflective professional input

We want depth, but not amateur therapy language.

4. Easier to answer consistently

The old questions were intelligent, but different staff could interpret them very differently.

The new questions should be:

simpler

tighter

more concrete

easier to answer in a consistent way across different respondents

That improves data quality across the whole platform.

5. More useful for story generation and reporting

Old questions may produce vague answers.
New questions are designed to produce:

specific moments

repeatable behaviour patterns

growth needs

clearer developmental insights

That makes the responses better for:

story personalisation

academy insight

parent summaries

player development profiles

The design rule going forward

Apply this rule to all coaching, psychology, and staff-facing questions:

Ask for observed behaviour, repeated patterns, and specific examples. Avoid asking respondents to guess private thoughts, assign deep causes, or make sweeping judgments about the child’s identity.

The practical rewrite standard

When rewriting every question, do these things:

Replace abstract wording

Instead of:

relationship with pressure

relationship with failure

inner dialogue

what shaped them

who they are underneath

Use:

what do you observe under pressure

how do they usually respond after mistakes

what behaviour or communication do you notice in difficult moments

what pattern have you seen over time

what situations tend to trigger this response

Replace interpretive phrasing

Avoid:

“what does this tell you about who they are?”

“what shaped them?”

“what is going on internally?”

Use:

“please share one example”

“what pattern have you noticed?”

“what tends to happen next?”

“in what situations does this appear most often?”

Keep the questions deep, but grounded

Do not make them shallow.
Keep the developmental depth, but make each question answerable through observation.

Example of the shift

Old style:
What is their relationship with failure, and what does that tell you about who they are?

New style:
How does this player usually respond after mistakes, setbacks, or disappointing performances? What pattern have you noticed over time?

What changed:

less abstract

less interpretive

more observable

more repeatable

easier to answer well

Another example:

Old style:
What does this player’s inner dialogue look like?

New style:
In difficult moments, what does this player appear to communicate to themselves through words, behaviour, or attitude?

What changed:

no mind-reading

still insightful

based on visible evidence

What I want you to do across the platform

Please review all existing coaching, psychology, parent, and staff-facing questions and rewrite them using this standard:

concrete over abstract

observed over assumed

example-based over theory-based

developmental over diagnostic

consistent over poetic

Tone standard for rewritten questions

The tone should be:

thoughtful

professional

warm

development-focused

non-clinical

not overly dramatic

Output I want from you

Please do this for all coaching and psychology questions in the app:

identify the existing question

rewrite it in the new standard

keep the same underlying intent

make it clearer, more observable, and more useful

where possible, ask for a specific example or repeated pattern
