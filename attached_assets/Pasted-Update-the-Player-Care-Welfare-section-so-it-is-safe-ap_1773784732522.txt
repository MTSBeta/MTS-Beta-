Update the Player Care / Welfare section so it is safe, appropriate, and aligned with the fact that we are writing a book and building a player-support profile — not collecting safeguarding case information.

This section may lightly explore support from home and the player’s wider environment, but it must NOT invite sensitive personal data.

Important safeguarding and privacy rules:
- Do not ask for safeguarding disclosures
- Do not ask for medical information or diagnoses
- Do not ask for trauma, abuse, neglect, or mental health history
- Do not ask for family conflict, finances, custody, or legal issues
- Do not ask staff to assess whether a home is “stable” or “unstable”
- Do not ask for confidential or highly personal background details
- Keep all questions general, observational, and suitable for a book-writing / player-development workflow
- Questions should focus on what helps us understand the player in a supportive, respectful, high-level way

The tone should be:
- warm
- child-centred
- observational
- strength-based
- non-clinical
- non-intrusive

Please rename or present this section in a safe way. You can keep the internal role as Player Care and Welfare, but the visible form language should feel lighter and less like a safeguarding report.

Use this section title:
Add Your Observations

Use this helper note at the top of the form:
Please keep responses general, supportive, and relevant to the player’s development. Do not include medical information, safeguarding disclosures, confidential family matters, or other highly personal details.

Use these questions exactly:

1. How does this player usually present at training or around the club environment?
You may comment on their mood, energy, confidence, or how settled they seem.

2. What helps this player feel comfortable, included, and able to engage well?
Please focus on what you have observed in the club environment.

3. How does this player tend to interact with staff and team-mates day to day?
Please share any positive patterns you have noticed.

4. What kind of support from home or outside the club seems to encourage this player most?
Please keep this high level and avoid private or sensitive details.

5. Are there any positive influences, routines, or values outside football that seem important to this player’s development?
For example, encouragement from family, role models, structure, or hobbies.

6. Have you noticed any positive changes in this player over time?
This could relate to confidence, communication, maturity, or engagement.

7. Is there anything important about how this player should be supported to help them feel at their best?
Please avoid including confidential or highly personal information.
